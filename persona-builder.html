Participant Information Sheet

Please read the following information carefully (scroll inside the box). When you are done, complete the Consent Form below.

Title of the research: Designing AI Communication Strategies Based on Situational Context and Worker Characteristics

Principal Investigator: Changbum Ahn (Professor, Department of Architecture and Architectural Engineering, College of Engineering, Seoul National University)

This research investigates how AI-generated safety warning messages should be designed for construction sites to ensure workers pay more attention to them and take safer actions. You are being asked to participate in the research because you are 18 years of age or older, have previous or current work experience on a construction site, and can understand and respond to a survey in English. A researcher at Seoul National University Changbum Ahn (Professor, Department of Architecture and Architectural Engineering, College of Engineering) and Mingi An (Ph.D. Candidate, Department of Architecture and Architectural Engineering, College of Engineering) will explain the research to you. The research will be conducted only with the voluntary consent of the participants, so before your decision is made it is important to understand the content and purpose of the research. Please read the content below carefully and thoroughly, and please let us know if you consent to be involved. If necessary, ask your family members or friends. If you have any question, the investigator will explain in detail.

1. Why is this research being conducted?

The purpose of this research is to identify how the design of AI-generated safety warning messages on construction sites affects workers' actual behavioral responses, and to propose design principles for AI safety communication that can operate adaptively according to situational context and worker characteristics.

2. How many people will participate in the research?

A total of 220 adults aged 18 or older, who have previous or current work experience on a construction site and can understand and respond to an English-language survey, will participate in this research.

3. How will the research proceed?

If you agree to participate, the research will proceed as follows.

  1. You will first complete a pre-survey. The pre-survey collects basic demographic information and information related to AI. Specifically, the demographic information includes your name, age, gender, country of residence, and highest level of education. The AI-related information includes your construction site work experience, whether you have ever used AI-based safety systems, and your level of trust in AI safety systems. You will be asked to enter or select the answers that apply to you.
  2. You will then see a photograph of a construction site. The image depicts a potentially hazardous situation that can occur on a construction site, accompanied by a written description of the situation. After viewing the image and reading the description, an AI-generated safety warning will be played as an audio message.
  3. After experiencing the construction site image, the description, and the corresponding AI-generated safety warning message, you will complete a short survey about what you experienced.
  4. First, you will respond to 3 questions that check your perception of the situation depicted in the image and the characteristics of the AI-generated safety warning message.
  5. Next, you will respond to 4 questions about the effectiveness of the AI-generated safety warning message.
  6. You will then see a new construction site photograph, different from the first one. This image also depicts a potentially hazardous situation that can occur on a construction site, accompanied by a written description. After viewing the image and reading the description, a corresponding AI-generated safety warning will be played as an audio message.
  7. The same procedure described in steps 3), 4), and 5) will be repeated for this second scenario.
  8. The entire survey is completed online through the provided link on your PC or mobile device. No offline, face-to-face contact or laboratory visit is required.

4. What is the duration of participation in the research?

It will take approximately 5~7 minutes.

5. Once participation in the research has begun, is it possible to stop participating?

Yes. You can stop participating in the research whenever you want with no disadvantage. Any time you want to stop, please tell the investigator immediately. If you withdraw, the data collected up to that point will be discarded immediately. Because this survey is conducted online, if you close the browser window, the entered content will not be collected.

6. Are there any side effects or risks involved in participation in this research?

This research is conducted as an online survey and does not involve any physical intervention; it is classified as minimal-risk research. The risks associated with participation are limited to the psychological burden typically associated with routine survey participation.

You may experience minor psychological discomfort during the survey. This can arise from viewing images of hazardous construction site situations (e.g., heavy equipment approach, missing personal protective equipment), which may cause a temporary sense of tension or discomfort for some participants. To minimize this, the images used depict only the possibility of a hazardous situation, not actual accident scenes, and the intensity of the scenarios has been moderated to avoid causing excessive fear or shock.

If you feel any psychological discomfort during the survey, you may stop participating at any time. If you have any questions about possible side effects or risks during the research, please contact the investigator immediately.

7. Are there any advantages to participating in the research?

You will not receive any direct advantage. However, the information you provide will be helpful to promoting understanding of AI communication on construction sites.

8. Are there any disadvantages to participating in the research?

You are free to agree or decline to participate in the research. There will be no disadvantage if you decide not to participate.

9. Is the information gathered during the participation secure?

The person in charge of managing the personal information collected for this research is Professor Changbum Ahn (Department of Architecture and Architectural Engineering, College of Engineering; Tel. 02-880-7064) at Seoul National University. The personal information collected in this study consists of your name, gender, age, country of residence, highest level of education, and construction industry experience. Among these, your name is collected as identifying information for the purpose of preventing duplicate participation and managing responses, and is also used to generate personalized messages during the survey; at the stage of data analysis, your name will be anonymized or excluded from the analysis so that you cannot be identified. Your gender, age, country of residence, highest level of education, and construction industry experience are collected as demographic information and will be used when analyzing the survey results. Such personal information is only allowed to be accessed by Changbum Ahn (Professor, Department of Architecture and Architectural Engineering, College of Engineering, Seoul National University) and Mingi An (Ph.D. Candidate, Department of Architecture and Architectural Engineering, College of Engineering, Seoul National University), and will be stored on an encrypted shared database within the principal investigator's laboratory (Construction Engineering & Management Laboratory, Seoul National University).

Documentation for the waiver of written informed consent will be retained for three years and then destroyed in accordance with Article 15, Paragraph 2 of the Enforcement Rule of the Bioethics and Safety Act. Research data excluding personally identifiable information will be mandatorily retained for five years in accordance with Article 16, Paragraph 3 of the Seoul National University Research Ethics Guidelines, and thereafter will continue to be stored for as long as the principal investigator is able to manage them to ensure research integrity.

All possible measures will be taken to secure and protect all personal information gathered while proceeding with this research. Research data excluding personally identifiable information may be used for research purposes other than this study, including academic conference presentations and journal publications by the principal investigator and other researchers. When the research is reported in an academic journal or presented at a conference, your name and other personal information will not be presented. However, if required by law, your personal information may be provided. In addition, the Seoul National University Institutional Review Board may directly access the research results within the scope of the relevant regulations to inspect whether the research has been conducted without the participant's personal information security and verify the reliability of the research data. By checking the agreement box, you acknowledge that you have been informed of all necessary information related to the research in advance and will be deemed to have consented to it.

10. How much will participants be paid for participation?

You will receive USD 1 (approximately KRW 1,500) as a token of appreciation for your participation. However, participants who withdraw partway through the study will not receive compensation.
